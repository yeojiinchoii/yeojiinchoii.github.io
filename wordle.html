Intro —

Background

Wordle is a popular online word-guessing game that challenges players to guess the correct five-letter word within six attempts each day. As part of our coursework, my two classmates and I, as a team, were assigned to design or enhance a game and identified Wordle as a potential option for improvement despite its existing popularity.

Understand —

Audience

WORDLE's target audience includes individuals who enjoy word-based games and have an interest in language-related activities. They may be motivated to improve their vocabulary and spelling skills or learn new words. Educators and students can also benefit from WORDLE as a supplement to language lessons. Additionally, those who enjoy competition and tracking their progress over time may find the game engaging.

Problems

We tested by playing WORDLE games available in the market to gain a deeper understanding of the user experience. During this process, we identified and reframed pain points as opportunities for improvement. Given our limited timeframe, we needed to establish a reasonable scope for our design and development efforts. As a result, we categorized essential and non-essential features, establishing clear goals and non-goals, to ensure that we could deliver a high-quality user experience within the given constraints.

Define —

Goals

Our goal was to create designs that were not only visually appealing but also functional, responsive, and user-friendly. As a team, we strived to collaborate effectively and deliver exceptional results within a limited time frame.

"How might we
help a user have a few minutes of joy?"

Research —

Competitive Analysis

The aim of this competitive analysis was to assess the strengths and weaknesses of our competitors to identify potential market opportunities. The analysis also provided insights on how to improve our game strategy. However, we had to be mindful of our development capabilities and consider the scope of the project given the timeline.

User Flow

This is a simplified/basic version in 5 steps that reflects the solutions for this scope. More complex user flow may have additional steps and features for next scope.

Information Architecture

I created this IA to design the structure of the web app with the goal of improving usability and helping users find what they need easily.

Design —

Wireframes

Before building the actual interface of the Wordle web app, I created wireframes to map out the user flow and the general layout of the app. This helped to identify potential issues and refine the design before proceeding to the actual development phase, but it also allowed us to set a scope for our team.

Creative Considerations

In this project, we focused on addressing three major pain points. Based on these pain points and their solutions, we aimed to make design elements such as font, colours, and patterns more clear. We tested colours that meet the WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) and considered the patterns for colour blindness.

Testing

We considered many different scenarios that users can face. For examples, display appropriate error message, and what is next after typing any random words etc.

Solution Design

I designed clear and concise instructions, an intuitive "Enter" button, recognizable patterns, and mobile responsiveness. These design choices were made to ensure a smooth and enjoyable user experience.

Reflections —

Learnings

As a member of the project team, it was a great practice and opportunity for me to plan and manage tasks and organize collaboration meetings. I also facilitated a UX workshop, and created wireframes and UI designs. I also worked on HTML and CSS, documented our progress, and conducted QA testing.

One important lesson I learned was the significance of understanding our users. Despite being a simple game, there were multiple opportunities to improve it for different users. However, we had to compromise some features due to the limited time and our development capabilities. Overall, it was a valuable experience in enhancing my skills in project management, design, and development.
