Brief —

Background

Our team was tasked by Google to create an interactive experience for their APAC Leadership event. We revamped the 'Insights Pump,' which is a digitally-connected bicycle pump that enables users to inflate a 3D object on the screen by pumping its handle. The inflated object then reveals key insights in an engaging way. By using this physical-to-digital interaction, we created an immersive experience that captivated event attendees and effectively communicated important information.

My Role

I was responsible for providing art direction, creating wireframes, and designing the user interface to seamlessly connect the pump and screen. I then handed these designs over to the development team for implementation.

Approach —

Design Approach

For a past project, we were tasked with updating the Insights Pump Experience used at the 2019 event to align with the Google 2022 Brand standards. To achieve this, we incorporated bold iconography and layouts, while focusing on a yellow and blue colour palette to ensure a distinctly Google feel.

3D approach

A key aspect of the Insights Pump experience is the surprise and delight of seeing a 3D object inflate and reveal an insight as it floats away. To achieve this, we drew inspiration from the Google 2022 Brand photography style and applied careful consideration to texture to ensure the objects were clearly recognizable as inflatables. Colours were chosen from the Google brand palettes, and a tone-on-tone approach was utilized for a cohesive look.

Design —

Wireframes

We used questions accompanied by illustrations to motivate our design process, and we implemented a new visual style by utilizing temporary 3D objects as placeholders.

Inflatable objects (Insight 1)

Topic: Connected TV/Online Video
Inflatable options could represent the technology involved with TV or perhaps something related to a specific category of content.

Inflatable objects (Insight 2)

Topic: Innovation / AI / Machine Learning
Here we explored gaming artefacts or more quirky representations of AI and machine learning - a robot or brain.

Testing

When I was designing the final interface, the development team created a prototype which we tested throughout the process. We were able to identify and fix a few issues, such as object movement and positioning. Testing also provided me with a better understanding of the app's functionality and how the pump interacts with the design.

Solution Design

The solution for the Google Insight Pump project involved designing an interactive experience that aligned with the Google 2022 Brand standards. I utilized bold iconography, blue and yellow palettes, and 3D objects that inflated and revealed insights.

Results —

Business Impact

The Insights Pump, a digitally-connected bicycle pump that inflates 3D objects on screen, has been a hit at multiple Google forums and seminars. It has been reissued with new graphics to keep the wacky invention fresh and engaging.
