Intro —

Background

Debitsuccess is a billing company based in Auckland, New Zealand and part of the Xplor group. During my time at Debitsuccess, I worked on several projects, but I have chosen to highlight two related ones: the Self-Service portal and the Customer Portal.

The Self-Service portal is used by facility staff and members to manage their customers, while the Customer Portal is mandatory for TSG (now Xplor) customers with payments after six months of subscription. The Customer Portal was not yet available when I joined the team, so we had to build it to meet a mandatory requirement for customers, while also improving the service for our clients.

The Self-Service portal was already in use by clients in health and fitness, insurance, education, and childcare. However, it was difficult to use and not intuitive. The Customer Portal, on the other hand, allows Debitsuccess customers to view their transaction history and edit their information for gym memberships or childcare payments.

Understand —

Audience

Debitsuccess has two primary audiences: facility staff/members (e.g., gym managers) who use the Self Service platform to manage their customers, and customers (e.g., individuals who go to the gym) who use the Customer Portal to view their transaction history and edit their information for gym memberships or childcare payments after six months of subscription.

Problems

Some pain points identified in the UX audit and defined exiting persons for the Debit Success online portal called ‘Self Service’ this web app include outdated design, lack of clear hierarchy in information, and no search functionality for clients to easily find what they are looking for. Additionally, the use of technical jargon and unclear language can lead to confusion for users.

Define —

Goals

Our UX team developed business goals, user goals, and user tasks for Debitsuccess' digital products, which were then broken down into manageable tasks for iteration and maintenance in agile development.

From the requirements and goals, our UX team validated features and prioritized tasks for 5 sprints of the Program Increment (PI) planning process, with each sprint lasting 8-10 days. During that time, we held several user journey workshops with teams, including the account manager, customer service, business analysts, the project manager, product owners, and stakeholders.

"How might we provide an intuitive and modern design for the Debitsuccess digital products?"

Personas

Before I joined the UX team, I had to define the existing personas in different industries and internally for the redesign of the Self-Service Portal. Frankly, we were looking for a good spot where everyone wouldn't miss them, so we decided to put these personas next to the bathroom/washroom in the office to raise awareness with all teams of how these personas are important for us to improve our digital products.

Information Architecture for the Self-Service portal

The IA for the Self-Service portal involved defining the site structure and content organization to ensure a seamless user experience. This included scoping the goals and identifying pain points, as well as conducting user testing to inform the IA design. The resulting IA design was aimed at improving the usability and intuitiveness of the Self-Service Portal, making it easier for facility staff and members to manage their customers.

Brand Personality Workshop

Our UX team conducted a Brand Personality Workshop to define the brand's voice, tone, and personality with several internal teams. This workshop aimed to ensure our brand consistency and a seamless user experience across all touchpoints. By establishing the brand's unique attributes, values, and characteristics, we were able to create a strong and distinctive brand identity that resonated with our target audience. The result was a modern, personable and friendly brand that effectively communicated its message, improved brand awareness, and increased customer trust.

Here are the results of the brand personality workshop for Mr. Debitsuccess (DS). The majority of participants chose a 'smart casual' style for the brand's personality. In the spectrum mapping exercise, we gained valuable insights that will guide us in building a unique personality for DS products. The mapping shows that 'Personable' and 'Corporate' were equally represented, and 'Careful Thinking' was rated higher than 'Energy.' Additionally, 'Modern' was preferred over 'Classic,' and 'Cutting Edge,' 'Fun,' and 'Accessible to All' were found to be closer to Mr. DS's personality.

Research —

Competitive Analysis

By comparing the Debitsuccess product to other competitors, it identified areas where improvements could be made and areas where it excels. This process also provided valuable insights into market trends and user expectations, which helped make better design decisions that meet the needs of the Debitsuccess target audience.

User Interviews

To ensure that we were creating a user-friendly product, we conducted user interviews and gained some really valuable feedback and insights.

Design Sprint

To generate additional ideas and insights, we conducted a design sprint involving stakeholders, account managers, business analysts, and product owners. This sprint focused on a different page in the Debitsuccess product for our clients.

Information Architecture for the Cutomer portal

Usability Testing

Our UX team conducted a usability testing session in order to identify problems by gathering feedback directly from real users, rather than making assumptions from behind a desk. We recruited 5 internal testers to try out both the old and new versions of the product, using scenarios designed to test specific user goals, such as 'You are a gym receptionist and a customer has requested to cancel their account,' 'Transfer a customer's account to another facility,' and 'Make a payment,' among others.

Creative Considerations

I led the creation of a design system with reusable UI components using React for Debitsuccess digital products, as there were no design guidelines in place. I worked with a development team to create guidelines, documentation, and testing, and organized introduction sessions for collaboration tools like Zeplin and Figma. We eventually transitioned to Figma for its design and prototyping capabilities. Throughout the project, I focused on consistency and accessibility in design elements, such as font, colors, and patterns.

Results —

Business Impact

I can't share specific details of the YouTube Delivery Korea 2021 project results due to confidentiality. But based on measurable indicators, it successfully achieved its goals of connecting YouTube with key partners and providing a more engaging experience for marketers. The project was well-received, likely strengthening YouTube's relationships with partners in South Korea. It also demonstrated YouTube's commitment to innovation and adaptability during the pandemic. We received positive feedback internally and externally, and it was a great opportunity to work with talented individuals on the project.
