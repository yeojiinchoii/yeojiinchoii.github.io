Intro —

Background

I love this brand. Also, I had already seen a lot of Blunt umbrellas on the street in Auckland City, New Zealand, but I didn’t know the name of the brand. I was wondering about the name because the shape of the umbrella was so unique, with rounded corners. When I eventually heard about working with the manufacturer, Blunt, I was so happy.

A brief summary of the Blunt Umbrellas project is here. It is a remarkable company that is currently expanding worldwide. They aim to enhance the efficiency and profitability of their e-commerce website and establish advertising methods that can replicate their local triumphs in new markets. I was lucky enough to have a supportive team, and managers and clients who helped me throughout this project. The task was to design a landing page based on user behaviour.

Define —

Find the right problems

By observing user behaviors through a tool called Inspectlet, I was able to identify user needs and problems that required attention. One particular issue I found was that many mobile users struggled to see colour changes on the product display after selecting a colour because the colour options were located outside of the mobile screen size. This caused them to have to scroll up and down repeatedly to view the colour changes.

Additionally, we received user feedback that revealed another issue. Many users were unknowingly ordering small-sized umbrellas without realizing the actual size. This was because they tended to avoid more expensive items, which resulted in them receiving a smaller-sized umbrella.

Research —

Competitive Analysis

To evaluate our competitors' strengths and weaknesses, I conducted a competitive analysis where I examined three applications offering services similar to Blunt's. Afterward, I created a SWOT analysis for each competitor to gain an in-depth understanding of their position in the market.

Persona

In order to gain a better understanding of our users, I developed a persona that serves as a reference for identifying users’ pain points and behaviors. This allows me to create more effective content that resonates with our users' needs and preferences.

Mind map

To improve the usability of our landing page, I analyzed the existing content and reorganized it to ensure that it provides helpful information. After compiling a list of features that we wanted to include on the landing page, I sorted them into a content hierarchy to ensure that the most important information is easily accessible to visitors.

Design —

Wireframes

I created wireframes for both mobile and desktop views to begin developing the page's structure. These wireframes served as a foundation for outlining the visual and typographic hierarchy, as well as organizing the overall interface of the page. Through the wireframing process, I was also able to identify the essential features needed for our users, ensuring that the final product would be both functional and user-friendly.

Testing

Before launching the landing page, we conducted thorough functionality testing, including readability, images, tables, clickability, and orientation for responsive website design. We also prioritized the most important features during the testing phase, ensuring they were easy to use and functioning properly. This approach allowed us to launch a high-quality landing page that met the needs of our users and our project goals.

Solution Design

This is the solution design that addresses several issues for users. Firstly, it ensures that colour changes are visible without requiring users to scroll up and down the page. Secondly, a sticky navigation feature enables users to add items to their cart and view pricing and reviews at any point in the checkout process. Finally, to cater to users' preference for visual information, I included images of umbrella sizes that are easily scannable.


Results —

Business Impact

As part of this project, we not only changed the user experience and user interface, but also started to treat different customer segments differently. The Double marketing team personalized the buying experience for various customer groups based on their age, income, location, and buying behaviour to maximize conversion. Additionally, the team implemented a weather-based ads automation system that triggered ads during bad weather, such as rain and wind, which significantly increased the chances of people visiting the website and making a purchase. It was great teamwork and as a result of these efforts, we achieved a remarkable 300% increase in website conversion rates and received great feedback from the client.
